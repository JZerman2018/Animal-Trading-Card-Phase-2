Grey Reef Shark

Grey Reef Sharks are well known in the scientific community for their hunch threat display when they are ready to attack.

  • Scientific Name: Carcharhinus Amblyrhynchos
  • Average Length: 5 - 6.6 ft
  • Average Lifespan: Up to 25 years
  • Habitat: Near Coral Reefs
    •

Grey reef sharks are grey in color and live near coral reefs. They feed on bony fishes, cephalopods, and crustaceans. They can reach about 6.6 feet in length, and can live up to 25 years in the wild. They are threatened by commercial fishing since they frequently get tangled in fishing nets. Scientists believe they may become endangered in the near future.
